Bristol Racing

A student-led team entering the Greenpower Formula 24+ competition: the aim being to blueprint, build, and endurance race a small electric car.

Introduction for Freshers

Welcome to Bristol Racing!

As a fresher, this is your opportunity to gain hands-on engineering experience, work with a passionate team, and develop skills that will set you apart in the industry.

The team is divided into four key sub-teams, each playing a crucial role in getting the car competition-ready:

Aero Team

The aero team optimizes the car’s aerodynamics to improve performance and handling, designing wings, diffusers, and other aerodynamic elements.

Key Skills Utilised by the Team:

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) Simulations
  • Wind Tunnel Testing & Validation
  • Drag Reduction & Downforce Optimisation

Composites Team

Responsible for lightweight, high-strength components like the monocoque and bodywork, the composites team ensures the car is structurally efficient.

Key Skills Utilised by the Team:

  • Carbon fiber layups & resin infusion
  • Mould design & CNC machining
  • Structural optimisation & failure analysis
  • Material testing & quality control

Electrical Team

The electrical team focuses on the car’s electronics, wiring, and power distribution. They ensure all sensors, controls, and safety systems function seamlessly.

Key Skills Utilised by the Team:

  • PCB design & circuit debugging
  • Soldering & assembling electrical components
  • Working with sensors & data logging
  • Programming for control systems (Embedded C, MATLAB, Simulink)

Mechanical Team

This team designs and builds the car’s chassis and suspension, ensuring structural integrity and performance.

Key Skills Utilised by the Team:

  • CAD & FEA (Fusion 360, SolidWorks, ANSYS)
  • Structural analysis & material selection
  • Suspension geometry & vehicle dynamics
  • Manufacturing processes (milling, turning, welding)
